screen-real-estate notes.

1
Inline tappable time spans cost zero extra space. The "25 min" text was already in step 3 — we just render it as a terracotta pill instead of plain prose. Same character count. Same line height. The pill background does the work of saying "I'm interactive."
2
The sticky timer bar only appears when something is running. Zero timers = zero added UI. Frame 2 (timer about to start) has no timer bar — the body content starts right under the app bar. Frame 1 (two timers active) shows the bar takes about 44 px including padding. About one screen-line's worth, easy to dismiss visually if you don't care, easy to read at a glance if you do.
3
Active vs paused state is visual on both surfaces. The inline pill in step 3 turns mocha-grey with a pause glyph when the timer is paused (step 4 in Frame 1). The chip in the top bar follows the same convention. Tap either to toggle pause / resume. Long-press cancels.
4
Steps with no parseable time get a subtle clock icon at the right edge (40% opacity grey — see step 2 in Frame 2). Tappable, opens the same duration picker but with no pre-fill. Almost invisible until you look for it, but always there.
5
The picker is a bottom sheet, not a full screen. Quick chips for common durations + "custom…" for manual entry. The detected duration ("25 min") is pre-selected. One tap → start. Two taps if they want to change it. The recipe stays visible behind a dim scrim so they don't lose context.
6
When a timer fires, it fires regardless of mode. Sound + vibrate via flutter_local_notifications — works when the app is backgrounded, when the screen is off, when the user is in another app. The chip in the top bar turns terracotta-flash and shows "DONE — tap to dismiss." Hands-free voice mode integration is a v1.1 follow-up; the in-mode firing experience is identical because it's the same notification.
