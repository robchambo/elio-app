scope adjustment.

Yummly is dead. Whirlpool acquired it in 2017 for ~$100M, laid off the entire team in April 2024, and shut the app down on 20 December 2024. There was no bulk export — many users with hundreds of saved recipes lost everything. The original brief named Yummly as a key competitor; we've swapped in Plan to Eat, which actively targeted the Yummly migration with recipe-import tooling and a 30%-off promo. The Yummly story itself is a useful data point, captured below.

the yummly timeline (cautionary tale)

2017
Whirlpool acquires Yummly for a reported $100M. Big bet on AI-personalised cooking + smart-appliance tie-in.
Apr 2024
Entire Yummly team laid off as part of Whirlpool's "operating model adjustment". App enters maintenance mode.
Nov 2024
Discontinuation notice sent to users. No bulk recipe export — download one at a time before the deadline.
18 Dec 2024
Yummly Smart Thermometer bricked. Whirlpool offers partial Visa rebates ($30–$87).
20 Dec 2024
Yummly app and website permanently shut down. ~5M+ users displaced; Plan to Eat, Paprika, Mealime absorb the migration.
Lessons
(1) Bulk export must exist at launch — trust signal. (2) Hardware-parented apps carry brand risk — if the parent pivots, the app dies. (3) The largest AI-personalised recipe app couldn't sustain itself under a hardware company. Category economics matter.

what elio already owns.

Three capabilities that no competitor in the set combines. These are the moat — protect them, lead with them in messaging.

★
AI generation grounded in your actual pantry
DishGen takes ingredient lists. Samsung Food does Vision AI for ordering. Mealime, Paprika, AnyList don't generate at all. Nobody combines pantry + perishable expiry + dietary union into recipe generation.
★
Receipt OCR plus barcode plus expiry urgency
Samsung Food does barcode + Vision AI from photos. No competitor does receipt OCR. Elio's "what expires today gets cooked tonight" loop is a clean run.
★
Household dietary union math
Mealime supports a profile. Plan to Eat and AnyList have meal planning. None combine multiple humans' dietary restrictions and allergies into a single weekly plan. Best fit for mixed-diet families.

the five must-match gaps.

Every category competitor has these. Reviews mention them. Launching without them will produce 1- and 2-star "missing basics" reviews on day one.

1
Real household sharing with email/link invite AnyList household ($14.99/yr), Bring!, OurGroceries, Samsung Food, Plan to Eat all let multiple humans edit the list from their own devices in real time. Elio's "household members under one UID" means only the account holder edits. Single highest-leverage feature for retention.
launch blocker
2
In-app cooking timers + cook mode Paprika and SideChef both ship multi-timer cook mode and reviews repeatedly cite it as the reason to stay. Elio has voice cooking but no timer widget. 2–3 days of work, big reviewer payoff.
launch blocker
3
Browseable saved-recipe library + collections Every recipe app has some form of saved-recipe browse-and-organise. Elio has saved history with collection tags but it isn't merchandised. Mealime users complain "running out of variety"; Yummly migrants asked for this loudly. Mostly a UI repackaging of existing data.
launch blocker
4
Apple Watch + voice-assistant add-to-list Bring!, AnyList, OurGroceries all have "Hey Siri / Hey Alexa, add milk" plus a Watch companion. The literal-most-requested feature for any shopping list. Elio has neither.
launch blocker
5
User-customisable aisle ordering Plan to Eat owns this with per-store layouts ("my Trader Joe's"). AnyList and OurGroceries let users edit categories. Elio's auto-classification is ~10 fixed aisles. Reviews tie this directly to long-term stickiness.
launch blocker

per-app teardowns.

Verbatim quotes are real, sourced from the App Store, Play Store, JustUseApp, Plan to Eat's review blog, and 2026 review aggregators (see Sources). Italics indicate direct user language.

Paprika

$4.99 mobile / $29.99 desktop · one-time
"The dependable, no-subscription, power-user recipe library. Buy once, own forever."

Standout features

  • Web clipper from any URL (champion)
  • Drag-drop weekly/monthly meal planner
  • Smart shopping list with auto-deduplication
  • Multiple cooking timers
  • "Cook mode" (screen-on)
  • Serving-size scaling

What users complain about

Per-app, per-device pricing — phone, tablet, desktop = three separate purchases.App Store / 2026
The calendar is just a calendar — adding a recipe to the meal plan doesn't add the ingredients to the shopping list.Plan to Eat review / 2023
Lose all changes (crossed-off items, highlighted steps) if you navigate to a different section.App Store
For elio: Paprika's UI is dated and the meal-plan/shopping-list disconnect is a clear opportunity — elio's auto-merge is already better. Don't compete on "buy once" pricing — AI APIs make it unsustainable. Lift Paprika's cooking-timer pattern.

Mealime

Free / $5.99 mo / $49.99 yr
"30-minute weeknight dinners, automatic shopping list. For busy professionals."

Standout features

  • ~1,200 curated recipes, all 30-min
  • Step-by-step photo guidance
  • Dietary filter (vegan/keto/etc.)
  • Aisle-sorted grocery list
  • Weekly meal plan

What users complain about

When you filter for dietary restrictions, remove ingredients you dislike, and exclude what you've already made — the pool shrinks fast. Same meals every few weeks.App Store / 2025-2026
The app doesn't know what's in my kitchen, doesn't remember what I cooked last week, and can't generate anything new.Mealime community forum
Most-requested change in the community forum: generate new recipes based on what I want.Mealime community forum
For elio: The Mealime user base is the ideal Elio audience. Their dominant complaint ("can't generate, repeats forever") is precisely Elio's positioning. Highest-fidelity messaging target.

SideChef

Free / $4.99 mo / $49.99 yr
"Step-by-step interactive cooking with voice. Closest thing to a video cookbook + smart timer."

Standout features

  • 18,000+ "interactive smart recipes"
  • Voice-guided step navigation
  • Step photos and videos
  • Multiple cooking timers in-line
  • Partner-chef classes (Premium)

What users complain about

iPad version doesn't rotate — clearly a blown-up iPhone app.App Store
Step image from previous instruction shows for the current step.App Store
Recipes no longer save into the grocery list. Reinstall didn't fix it.App Store
Account deletion request ignored.JustUseApp / 2025
For elio: SideChef's voice + step-photo cook mode is the bar Elio's voice cooking is being measured against. The QA reputation is so bad that "works reliably" is itself an opportunity. Don't chase the 18k recipe corpus — own the polished cook screen instead.

DishGen

Free 15 credits/wk / $7.99 mo / $15.99 mo Pro
"Generate a recipe from ingredients in 60 seconds, refine in chat."

Standout features

  • Free-text or ingredient-list input
  • Dietary filtering
  • Chat-based refinement
  • Recipe book creation
  • Adaptive personal AI model (Premium)

What users complain about

Recipes vary significantly in quality — some clearly raw output that nobody has cooked.2026 review aggregator
Hallucinations: ingredients that didn't exist. Some recipes appear stolen directly from existing sources like Minimalist Baker.NPR / 2024
Cottage cheese breadstick recipe resulted in "an eggy sheet with herbs" instead of twisted breadsticks.Futurism / 2024
The company has not verified it for accuracy or safety.DishGen own disclaimer
For elio: DishGen's failure mode is exactly Elio's existential risk. Defence: pantry/expiry grounding makes recipes more concrete; Gemini 2.5 Flash quality > generic LLM. Add a private "did this work?" feedback loop to flush bad recipe templates fast. Never publish a community library of Elio outputs without verification — that's the line DishGen crossed.

five messaging angles.

Five claims Elio can defend with verbatim competitor-review evidence. Specific and competitive — not "great recipes" (which everyone claims).

CLAIM 01
Recipes that use what's actually in your kitchen — including what's about to go off.
Mealime users: "the app doesn't know what's in my kitchen, doesn't remember what I cooked last week, and can't generate anything new." No competitor pulls expiring perishables into recipe generation as a hard constraint.
CLAIM 02
Different every week. No more cycling through the same five dinners.
Mealime users: "same meals every few weeks." Mealime's most-requested community feature ("generate new recipes based on what I want") is exactly elio's core flow.
CLAIM 03
Your recipes don't disappear when we update.
Yummly users (post-shutdown): "lost years of saved recipes overnight." Samsung Food: "lost recipes when syncing between devices." Paprika: "some go missing during updates." Low bar — elio's Firestore + bulk export lets us own it.
CLAIM 04
One plan, the whole household — allergies and diets included.
No competitor in the set does household dietary union math. Mealime supports a single profile but won't combine multiple humans' restrictions. Best fit for mixed-diet families.
CLAIM 05
Hands-free cooking that actually works.
SideChef has voice but reviewers flag QA: "step image from previous instruction shows for the current step." Elio's voice mode is on par feature-wise; reliability is the differentiator. Verify on-device before claiming.

features elio should not build.

Several features show up in competitors but fight Elio's positioning. Decide once, document, and move on.

Public recipe library / community feedYummly tried. DishGen does it badly (stolen recipes). Moderation cost is huge. Dilutes the "AI generates for your pantry" value.
Smart-fridge integrationSamsung Food's moat. Irrelevant for US-priority launch. Defer indefinitely.
18,000-recipe browseable corpusSideChef's moat. Elio is generation-first; a library distracts from the differentiation. Personal saved library is fine.
One-time pricing per platformPaprika model. Firestore + Gemini API recurring costs make it unsustainable for AI.
Step photos for generated recipesGenerated recipes can't have authentic cooking photos. Stock or AI-generated images would erode trust.
Calorie/macro tracking with daily targetsMyFitnessPal territory. Different audience, harder to win. Per-recipe nutrition only.
Coupons / store flyers / price trackingAdjacent category (Flipp, Ibotta). Mood-killer for "what should I cook tonight."
