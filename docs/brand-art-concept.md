# Elio — Brand & Art Concept (one-pager)

**For:** Brand + art direction pass with Kate
**Status:** Pre-launch (Sprint 15.3.19) — coordinated Android + iOS launch

---

## What Elio is (in one line)
An AI recipe generator that already knows what's in your kitchen — so cooking starts with "what shall I make?", not "what do I need to buy?".

## Who it's for
Busy households (couples, families, flatshares) who want to eat well without the daily friction of meal planning, list-making, and "what do we even have in?". US primary, UK secondary.

## Core promise
**Remove friction.** Minimal taps. Simplicity over completeness. Every screen earns its place.

---

## Brand personality

| Is | Isn't |
|---|---|
| Warm, calm, capable | Loud, gimmicky, "tech-bro" |
| Knowing — like a friend who cooks | Preachy or aspirational lifestyle |
| Practical, grounded, household-real | Restaurant-chef, foodie-elitist |
| Quietly clever (the AI fades into the background) | "Powered by AI!" sticker energy |
| Inclusive of all skill levels and diets | Diet-culture, guilt-driven |

**Voice:** plain English, second person, short sentences. Helpful flatmate, not a sommelier. Light dry humour is fine; never twee. Never exclamation marks in body copy.

---

## Visual system (current)

**Palette**
- **Navy** `#1A2744` — primary surfaces, trust, calm
- **Amber** `#F08C14` — single accent, CTAs, "Elio is doing something"
- **Sky** `#4A90D9` — secondary accent, info states
- **Off-white** `#F7F5F2` — backgrounds, breathing room

One accent (amber) does *all* the heavy lifting — generate buttons, primary actions, the trial paywall hero. Keeps the eye trained.

**Typography**
- **Outfit** — headings (geometric, modern, friendly)
- **Quicksand** — body (rounded, approachable, legible at small sizes)

**Shape language**
Rounded corners throughout. Soft cards. No hard edges, no skeuomorphism. Shimmer skeletons during streaming so the app *feels* alive while Gemini thinks.

---

## What needs Kate's eye

1. **Logo / wordmark** — do we have a settled mark? The amber is doing brand work but there's no symbol carrying it. An "E" mark, a pantry-jar glyph, or a flame/ember pun on "Elio" are all on the table.
2. **Illustration style** — empty states (empty pantry, no recipes yet, onboarding completion screen) currently lean on icons + emoji. A single consistent illustration style would lift the whole app.
3. **App icon** — needs to read at 48px on a cluttered home screen. Amber on navy is the obvious play; the question is *what shape*.
4. **Onboarding hero art** — 8 onboarding screens currently use icons + progress bar. One signature illustration per screen would make the first run feel crafted instead of utilitarian.
5. **Photography vs illustration for recipes** — Gemini gives us text, not images. Do we generate hero images, illustrate them, or own the "no photo" aesthetic with strong typography + colour blocks? (My vote: own it. It's faster, cheaper, and on-brand.)
6. **Paywall art** — the trial-first paywall is the single most commercially important screen. It currently has a feature checklist and no imagery. Worth a dedicated piece.

---

## Constraints to design within
- **Mobile-first, Android-primary** — designs must hold up on cheap Android screens, not just iPhone hero shots.
- **Dark UI (navy) is the default** — illustrations need to live on a dark background.
- **No stock photography** — it ages badly and undermines the "knowing friend" voice.
- **Accessibility** — amber-on-navy contrast already passes; any new colour must too.
- **Single accent rule** — resist the urge to introduce a third action colour. If everything is highlighted, nothing is.

---

## The north star
Open Elio at 6pm on a Tuesday. Tap once. Get a recipe you can actually make tonight, with what's already in the cupboard. The brand should feel like that moment: a small, quiet relief.
