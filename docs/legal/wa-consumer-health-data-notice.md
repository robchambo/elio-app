## 2. What is "consumer health data" in Elio's context?

We treat the following data as **consumer health data** under RCW 19.373.010:

- **Dietary requirements** you tell us (e.g. vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, dairy-free, low-FODMAP, diabetic-friendly, kosher, halal);
- **Allergies and intolerances** you tell us (e.g. peanut allergy, lactose intolerance, shellfish allergy);
- The **dietary requirements you provide for household members** you've added to your account.

We treat this as health data because allergies and dietary requirements can reveal a consumer's "past, present, or future physical or mental health status" within the meaning of RCW 19.373.010(8)(b)(viii).

We **do not** knowingly collect any other category of consumer health data. Specifically, we do **not** collect: symptoms, diagnoses, medications, biometric measurements, reproductive or sexual-health information, mental-health information, gender-affirming-care information, treatment history, lab results, or precise geolocation data near healthcare facilities.

## 3. How we collect this data (sources)

You provide consumer health data **directly** by:

- Answering dietary/allergy questions during onboarding;
- Editing your profile in the app;
- Adding or editing household members' dietary requirements.

We **do not** infer dietary or health data from your usage, purchase history, or any third-party source.

---

## 4. Why we collect it (purposes)

We process consumer health data **only** for the following purposes:

1. **To personalise the AI's recipe and meal-plan suggestions** to your dietary needs and to avoid ingredients you've told us you can't or don't want to eat.
2. **To display your profile back to you** in the app so you can review and change your selections.

We **do not** process consumer health data for analytics, advertising, marketing, profiling for behavioural purposes, training AI models, research beyond delivering the service to you, or any other purpose not listed above.

---

## 5. Who receives consumer health data (sub-processors)

Consumer health data is shared **only** with the following sub-processors, and only for the purposes listed in §4:

| Sub-processor | Role | Why they receive consumer health data |
|---|---|---|
| **Google LLC / Google Cloud / Firebase (Cloud Firestore)** | Database hosting | To store your profile and household-member records securely so they sync across your devices |
| **Google LLC (Generative AI / Gemini API)** | AI model | To generate recipes that respect your dietary requirements; the dietary list (without your name or any directly identifying information) is included in each prompt |

These sub-processors process the data under contractual confidentiality and security obligations. We do not allow them to use consumer health data for their own purposes, training of their general AI models, or marketing.

## 6. We do NOT sell consumer health data

**We do not sell consumer health data** as "sale" is defined under RCW 19.373.010(20). We have not done so in the past, and we have no plans to do so. We do not exchange consumer health data for monetary or any other valuable consideration.

We **do not** disclose consumer health data to advertisers, data brokers, or any third party for advertising or marketing purposes.

## 7. Your rights under MHMDA

You have the following rights with respect to your consumer health data:

| Right | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Right to confirm** | Confirm whether we are processing your consumer health data. |
| **Right to access** | Receive a copy of the consumer health data we hold about you, plus a list of all third parties (including affiliates) with whom we have shared it. |
| **Right to delete** | Have your consumer health data deleted. We will instruct our sub-processors to delete it as well. |
| **Right to withdraw consent** | Withdraw any consent you previously gave us for collection, use, or sharing of consumer health data. The withdrawal mechanism is at least as easy as the original consent. |

You have the right to exercise these rights **without facing discrimination** in the Service.

## 8. How to exercise your rights

To exercise any of the rights in §7, email us at:

**Email:** support@elio.app
**Subject line:** "**Washington health data request**"

Please include:

- Your account email address;
- Which right(s) you want to exercise;
- For deletion or access requests, any specific data categories you want covered.

### 8.1 Our response timeline

- We will respond to your request **within 45 days**.
- If we need more time, we may extend by a further 45 days, and we will notify you of the extension and the reasons for it within the original 45-day period.
- We will provide our response **free of charge**, except where requests are manifestly unfounded, excessive, or repetitive — in which case we may charge a reasonable administrative fee or refuse the request, and we will explain why.

### 8.2 Verification

To protect your data, we will verify your identity by matching the email address you contact us from against the email on your account. For deletion or access requests involving sensitive data, we may request one additional verification data point (for example, your account creation date or last sign-in device).

## 9. Right to appeal

If we deny your request (in whole or in part), you have the right to appeal.

**To appeal:** reply to our denial email with the subject line "**Washington health data appeal**". The appeal mechanism is at least as easy as the original request mechanism.

- We will acknowledge your appeal within **5 business days**.
- We will inform you of our final decision **within 45 days** of receiving the appeal, with a written explanation of the reasons.

**If we deny your appeal**, you may file a complaint with the **Washington State Attorney General**:

> **Washington State Attorney General — Consumer Protection Division**
> File a complaint online: **https://www.atg.wa.gov/file-complaint**
> Or call: **1-800-551-4636**

We will provide a working link to the AG's complaint mechanism in our denial email so the path forward is unambiguous.

## 10. Consent

We collect your **affirmative, opt-in consent** for the collection, use, and sharing of consumer health data through a dedicated in-app consent screen during onboarding, presented separately from acceptance of our general Privacy Policy and Terms of Service.

The consent screen explains:

- What categories of consumer health data we will collect (the list in §2);
- The purposes (the list in §4);
- The sub-processors who will receive the data (the list in §5);
- Your right to withdraw consent at any time, as easily as it was to provide.

### 10.1 Withdrawing consent

You can withdraw consent at any time by:

- Toggling the consent off in **Settings → Privacy → Consumer Health Data** (once that screen ships), or
- Emailing **support@elio.app** with the subject "**Withdraw health data consent**".

When you withdraw consent:

- We **stop further processing** of consumer health data immediately;
- We **delete** the consumer health data we already hold for you (and instruct our sub-processors to do the same), in line with §7's right to delete;
- The Service's **AI features that depend on dietary data become unavailable**, but the rest of the app continues to function.

Withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing that occurred before withdrawal.

## 11. Children

## 12. Security

We protect consumer health data using:

- **TLS encryption** for all data in transit between the app, our servers, and our sub-processors;
- **Encryption at rest** for data stored in Firebase Cloud Firestore;
- **Firestore Security Rules** that restrict each user's data to that user's own account — no other user can read or write your consumer health data;
- **Contractual safeguards** with each sub-processor listed in §5.

If we ever become aware of a data breach affecting your consumer health data, we will notify you and the Washington State Attorney General within the timelines required by Washington law (RCW 42.56.590 and RCW 19.255).
