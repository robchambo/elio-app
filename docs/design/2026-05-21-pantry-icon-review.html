2 · Proposed swaps

Spices & Seasonings

local_fire_department
Current — flame
local_fire_department_outlined
→
grass
Proposed — herb sprigs
grass_outlined

Why: the flame reads as spicy/heat rather than spice cupboard (which is mostly herbs, dry rubs, salt & pepper — not chillies). grass reads instantly as fresh herbs and generalises to dried herbs and seasonings without being literal about any one shape.

Indian Pantry

rice_bowl
Current — bowl & chopsticks
rice_bowl_outlined
→
kebab_dining
Proposed — skewer
kebab_dining_outlined

Why: the chopsticks-on-bowl visual is unambiguously Asian and clashes with the Asian Pantry tile right above it. kebab_dining reads as a tandoor skewer (chicken tikka, seekh kebab) — distinctly South-Asian without leaning on a stereotype.

Mexican & Latin

lunch_dining
Current — hamburger
lunch_dining_outlined
→
local_fire_department
Proposed — flame
local_fire_department_outlined

Why: the burger is North-American fast-food and reads wrong for Mexican & Latin. local_fire_department (freed up by the Spices swap above) maps cleanly onto chillies, tacos al pastor, and the general "heat" association of Mexican / Caribbean / Brazilian cuisines. Material has no chilli-pepper icon in the stock set.

Mediterranean

eco
Current — leaf
eco_outlined
→
wine_bar
Proposed — wine glass
wine_bar_outlined

Why: a leaf is too generic — could be any plant-forward cuisine. wine_bar evokes the Mediterranean dining culture (Italy/Greece/Spain/France) and pairs nicely with the existing local_bar-family iconography elsewhere in the app. If a wine glass feels off (e.g. dry / non-alcohol users), see alternative below.

Dairy & Eggs

egg
Current — teardrop
egg_outlined
→
egg_alt
Proposed — egg silhouette
egg_alt_outlined

Why: Material's egg_outlined draws an asymmetric teardrop that gets confused with the water_drop (Oils & Vinegars) and opacity (current Sauces) tiles next to it. egg_alt is the rounder, more egg-shaped variant — instantly readable, no shape collision with the liquid tiles.

Sauces & Condiments

opacity
Current — drop (≈ Oils)
opacity_outlined
→
liquor
Proposed — bottle
liquor_outlined

Why: opacity is a generic droplet — visually identical to water_drop used for Oils & Vinegars right above it. liquor draws a bottle silhouette (soy sauce, ketchup, hot sauce, Worcestershire all live in bottles), differentiates cleanly from the oil drop, and stays in the same monochrome family.

5 · Implementation

Single-file change — lib/widgets/pantry_builder_sheet.dart → _iconFor(String) switch (lines 139–168). Six case-arm updates. No asset bundling, no migration, no Firestore touch. The Pantry tab tile, the "+ Add" sheet tile, and the onboarding screens 11/12 category grids all read from the same function so the swap lands in every place icons are rendered.
